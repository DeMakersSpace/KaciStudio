04 — Usage Recommendations

01

Mahogany for dramatic moments

Use --mahogany for full-bleed dark hero sections or bold callout panels. Pair with --cream text and --accent for buttons — the warm red-brown against Polo Blue creates the strongest contrast in the palette.

02

Olive is decoration only

--olive (#B59E7D) fails contrast on all light surfaces. Restrict it to borders on dark sections, decorative dividers, or subtle icon fills. Never use it for body text or headings.

03

Blue is the only cool tone — protect it

--accent (Polo Blue) is intentional tension in a fully warm palette. Use it sparingly — CTAs, active nav dots, highlights. The moment it appears everywhere, it loses its power.

04

Consider upgrading --espresso to --mahogany

Currently --espresso and --fg are the same value (#584738). Reassigning --espresso to --mahogany (#4E342E) would give the banner and mobile menu a deeper, more dramatic feel — without touching any other component.

05

Dark surface layering order

When stacking dark surfaces, use this order from light to deep: --fg (#584738) → --nav-bg (#4A3B2C) → --mahogany (#4E342E). Note: nav-bg and mahogany are very close — avoid using both in the same view or they'll look like a mistake.

06

Alternate sections: bg → bg-alt → back

The two light surfaces (--bg and --bg-alt) are close enough to feel cohesive but distinct enough to visually separate sections without borders or shadows. Use them in alternating sequence.
