# KACISTUDIO — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Founders and brand managers of lifestyle, beauty, wellness, fashion, and F&B brands based in Singapore (some remote). Typically 6 months to 3 years into their brand — past the "just getting started" stage, ready to invest in how they show up. They arrive primarily via KACISTUDIO's Instagram link in bio or word-of-mouth referral, so they've likely seen the studio's work before landing on the site.

**Emotional state on arrival:** Quietly frustrated. They've probably worked with a generic agency or tried to handle socials themselves, and neither felt right — the content didn't sound like them. They're cautiously hopeful, not easily impressed.

**Job to be done:** Find a social media partner who already understands their brand story — before they've had to explain it three times. Not just an executor, but a creative collaborator.

---

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Thoughtful · Grounded · Soulful

**Voice:** Warm but not gushing. Confident but not loud. Poetic but not pretentious. Like a really good creative friend who also has a strategy brain — they get it before you finish the sentence.

**Tone rules:**
- Write like a person, not a brand
- Name feelings and frustrations before offering solutions
- Specificity over generality ("Two brands per quarter, max" over "we're selective")
- Avoid: corporate jargon, agency-speak, AI-flavoured sentences, anything that sounds like it was workshopped
- Never use: "synergy", "ROI-driven", "digital transformation", "raise awareness", "chaos", spaced dashes ( - ) in copy

---

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary reference (positive):** [getsocial.sg](https://www.getsocial.sg/) — specifically how work is displayed. Card-based editorial grid, high-quality imagery, each project stands independently, client names visible alongside visuals. Sophisticated neutral backdrop that lets the work breathe. Imagery-forward, not text-forward.

**Anti-reference:** [round3media.com](https://www.round3media.com) — described as "too childish." Avoid: bold primary-color circles as an organising system, overly playful/approachable-first tone, gimmicky visual motifs, personality-over-craft positioning, anything that prioritises "being Gen Z" over being genuinely good.

**Theme:** Light mode only. Warm cream base (`#F8F6DA`), not clinical white.

**Palette:**
- `--bg: #F8F6DA` — Coconut Cream (primary background)
- `--bg-alt: #F1EADA` — Merino (secondary sections)
- `--fg: #584738` — Judge Gray (body text)
- `--accent: #93A9D2` — Polo Blue (links, italics, highlights)
- `--espresso: #00145D` — Midnight Blue (dark section backgrounds, nav)
- `--olive: #B59E7D` — Mongoose (borders, warm accents)
- `--cream: #F8F6DA` — text on dark backgrounds

**Typography:**
- Display/Serif: Fraunces (SOFT axis, OPSZ range) — organic, editorial quality
- Body/UI: Geist — clean, contemporary, readable
- Labels/Mono: Geist Mono — technical precision in metadata and eyebrows
- Large headings: tight tracking (`letter-spacing: -0.03em to -0.04em`), line-height ~0.88–0.92
- Body: `line-height: 1.6–1.7`, `font-weight: 300`

**Texture & depth:** Grain overlay (`opacity: 0.028`) adds tactile warmth. Surfaces have a layering system: base cream → bg-alt → espresso dark.

**Motion:** Scroll reveal (`opacity 0` → `1`, `translateY(28px)` → `0`, `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)`). Marquee for social proof. Only `transform` and `opacity` animated — never layout properties.

---

### Design Principles

1. **Earn before you ask.**
   Every section should give value — insight, proof, or clarity — before asking for anything. The contact CTA should feel like a natural next step, not a push. Copy names the visitor's problem before offering the solution.

2. **Work speaks first.**
   Case studies and real results are the primary trust mechanism. The work section is not a gallery — each case should tell a story with a clear before/after. Show, then tell. Display should feel editorial, like getsocial.sg: imagery-forward, grid-based, each project with independent weight.

3. **Warm editorial, not cool minimal.**
   The cream palette, grain texture, and Fraunces serif should feel human and intentional — not decorative. Whitespace is generous but purposeful, never austere. Sections alternate between warm cream and midnight navy for rhythm, not just variety.

4. **Confidence through specificity.**
   Vague claims are replaced by specific truths. "Two new brands per quarter, max." "The person on your discovery call is the same one editing your reels." Specificity is what makes the brand feel real rather than performed.

5. **Design for relief, not excitement.**
   The emotional target is not "wow, this looks impressive" — it is "finally, someone who actually gets it." Every design choice should reduce friction and create recognition, not add wow-factor for its own sake. When a potential client reads a line of copy or sees a case study, they should feel seen.
